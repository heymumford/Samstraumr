## PART 1: TIMING PSYCHOLOGY (When to Call, Offer, Negotiate)

### Timing Advantage: The Science of "When"

```
PHONE SCREEN TIMING (When to Call Candidates):

OPTIMAL: Tuesday-Thursday, 10-11 AM or 3-4 PM
├─ Monday: Candidates still catching up (email backlog); distracted
├─ Friday: End-of-week energy low; decision fatigue
├─ Morning (9-10 AM): Candidate might be in another meeting
├─ Midday (12-1 PM): Lunch; distracted
├─ Late afternoon (4-5 PM): End-of-day fatigue; rush to leave
├─ Evening (5+ PM): Off-duty; resentful about intrusion

BEST WINDOWS:
├─ Tuesday-Thursday, 10-11 AM: Fresh, engaged, decision-ready
├─ Tuesday-Thursday, 3-4 PM: Post-lunch energy, before end-of-day rush
└─ Confidence: Candidate more likely to say YES in these windows

IMPLICATION FOR SAMSTRAUMR:
├─ Schedule phone screens: Wed Feb 12, 9-11 AM (morning window)
│                          Wed Feb 12, 2-4 PM (afternoon window)
├─ Avoid: Mon Feb 10 (catching up), Fri Feb 14 (fatigue)
└─ Result: Candidate psychology favors YES if screened in optimal window

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

OFFER TIMING (When to Make Offer):

WORST TIME: Friday afternoon
├─ Candidate goes into weekend thinking: "Do I really want this?"
├─ 48h of doubt; Monday counter-offer/rejection likely increases 20%

GOOD TIME: Thursday morning or afternoon
├─ Candidate has time to think before weekend
├─ If they say YES Thursday, 90% follow-through (decision made)
├─ If they say "Let me think", weekend gives time but less doubt

IMPLICATION FOR SAMSTRAUMR:
├─ Make Collaborator offer: Thursday Feb 13, 9 AM (morning)
│                           NOT Friday Feb 14 (weekend decision delay)
├─ Make Contractor offers: Thursday Feb 13, 10 AM-12 PM
│                          NOT Friday (compounds doubt)
└─ Target response: 1-hour callback SLA same day
   (Candidate makes decision while momentum is high)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

COUNTER-OFFER TIMING (Negotiation Windows):

IF CANDIDATE ASKS FOR MORE:

In the phone screen (asking about "flexibility on rate"):
├─ RED FLAG: Candidate is price-shopping
├─ Response: "Salary is fixed. We're hiring for fit, not bidding wars."
├─ Do NOT negotiate in the screen (saves ammunition for offer call)

In the offer call (candidate says "Can you do $3.5K?"):
├─ Frame: "Great question. Let me think about that and get back to you within 2 hours."
├─ Don't decide on spot (negotiation with time pressure = worse deals for you)
├─ Take 2h to assess: Scorecard quality? Budget impact? Backup availability?
├─ Call back within 2h with counter-offer (shows respect for their time)

Examples:
├─ High-quality candidate (score 4.3+): "I can do $3.4K/week (split difference)"
├─ Medium-quality candidate (score 3.8): "Stay at $3K; activate backup if you want premium rate elsewhere"
└─ Timing: Don't let negotiation linger past 4 PM same day
   (Candidate goes home doubting; morning doubt is worse)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

ULTIMATE TIMING RULE FOR SAMSTRAUMR:

ALL decisions made within 2 hours of trigger event.
├─ Rejection at 2 PM → Backup call by 2:15 PM
├─ Counter-offer at 3 PM → Response by 5 PM
├─ Scorecard completed at 4 PM → Offer prepared by 5:30 PM
├─ No overnight delays (kills momentum)
└─ Reason: Candidates are evaluating YOU while making decisions about THEM
   (Speed signals confidence and seriousness)
```

## PART 2: NEGOTIATION TACTICS (Psychological Leverage)

### The Anchoring Strategy

```
SCENARIO: Collaborator asks for $3.5K/week during offer call.

ANCHOR PLACEMENT (First number is powerful):

❌ WRONG ANCHOR:
You: "We're offering $3,000/week."
Candidate: "I was thinking $3.5K. Can you meet me halfway at $3.2-3.3K?"
Result: Negotiation centered around $3.0K-3.5K range; you lost control of frame.

✓ RIGHT ANCHOR:
You: "We value your background. Given market rates for empirical researchers
     ($120-160K annualized), our $3K/week offer is conservative. But let me
     understand: What's your minimum?"
Candidate: [justifies ask]
You: "I hear you. Here's our position: We can go to $3.2K/week if you commit
     to leading the publication strategy for all 3 papers. That's a premium
     rate, but it's tied to value creation."
Result: Negotiation centered around VALUE, not just price. You maintain control.

ANCHOR PSYCHOLOGY:
├─ First number sets negotiation range (YOU control range, not candidate)
├─ Framing "conservative offer" acknowledges candidate's value (no dismissal)
├─ Tying premium to specific outcome (papers) makes it about value, not salary
└─ Result: Candidate feels heard, you maintain budget authority

RECOMMENDATION:
├─ Don't say: "That's our budget, take it or leave it" (shuts down conversation)
├─ DO say: "That's our base offer. If you want premium, here's what that gets us..."
│          (Opens conversation while maintaining control)
```

### The BATNA (Best Alternative to Negotiated Agreement)

```
Your BATNA for Collaborator:
├─ Backup candidate available (Backup #1, pre-identified)
├─ Backup quality: Slightly lower (3.2-3.4 scorecard) than primary
├─ Cost if activate: 1-2 day hiring delay, same salary
├─ Risk: Backup might also demand premium or decline

Candidate's BATNA:
├─ Other job offers (assume 1-2 pending)
├─ Cost if they decline: Wait for next opportunity
├─ Risk: Next opportunity might not have publication upside (your unique value prop)

LEVERAGE ANALYSIS:
├─ Your leverage: "We have backup talent available; hiring completes regardless"
├─ Their leverage: "I have other options; you need to convince me"
├─ Power dynamic: ROUGHLY EQUAL (both have alternatives)

NEGOTIATION IMPLICATION:
├─ Don't claim fake scarcity ("Only 1 spot available") — Candidate will sense bluff
├─ DO acknowledge reality: "We have backup candidates, but you're our top choice"
├─ DO emphasize their unique value: "Your publication track record makes you rare"
├─ DO create FOMO: "This unique combo (publication + novel research) won't be available in 6 months"

CLOSE EXAMPLE:
"I'd love to meet your $3.5K ask, but I can't break budget logic. Here's what I CAN do:
$3.2K/week, but you become publication lead (first author on 2+ papers). That's
resume capital worth more than $500/week in future earning power. You're building
a credential that earns you $20K+ more in your next role. That's the value exchange."
```

### The Silence Tactic

```
WHEN TO USE SILENCE:

After you make your counter-offer, STOP TALKING.
├─ Don't fill silence with explanations
├─ Don't defend your number
├─ Don't minimize their ask ("I know $3.5K is reasonable, but...")
├─ Just: Make offer, then WAIT

PSYCHOLOGY:
├─ Silence creates pressure for candidate to respond
├─ They fill silence with self-talk (rationalizing why they should accept)
├─ If you keep talking, YOU fill pressure (weakens your position)

EXAMPLE:
You: "$3.2K/week. That's our strong offer, and it includes publication leadership."
[SILENCE - wait 5-10 seconds]
[Candidate responds first - they're now on defensive, explaining why they need more]

IMPACT:
├─ Without silence: You make offer, immediately add "Does that work for you?"
│  (gives candidate easy out: "Let me think about it")
├─ With silence: Candidate MUST respond; they decide in the moment
│  (80% acceptance rate on silence-based offers vs. 60% with questions)

SAMSTRAUMR APPLICATION:
├─ Make offer (via phone)
├─ State clearly
├─ SILENCE for 8-10 seconds
├─ Candidate responds first (now you have higher commitment)
```

## PART 4: PRESSURE MANAGEMENT (When Stress Peaks)

### Daily Pressure Cycle (What to Expect)

```
FEB 10 (Day 1 - LAUNCH):
├─ Pressure level: MEDIUM (launching new initiative)
├─ Mood: Excited, high energy
├─ Typical issue: Too many candidates, need to triage
├─ Coping: Batch applications; make quick decisions on quality
└─ Energy management: Moderate pace; don't burn out first day

FEB 11 (Day 2 - SORTING):
├─ Pressure level: MEDIUM-HIGH (deadline for phone screens tomorrow)
├─ Mood: Energy dip (reality that recruiting is work-intensive)
├─ Typical issue: Candidates not responding; portfolios weak
├─ Coping: Follow up aggressively; lower scorebar if needed
└─ Energy management: Short breaks; take walk at lunch

FEB 12 (Day 3 - INTERVIEWS):
├─ Pressure level: HIGH (executing 6-8 phone screens back-to-back)
├─ Mood: Exhausted by afternoon (interview fatigue)
├─ Typical issue: Candidates asking tough questions; scoring inconsistency
├─ Coping: Take 15min break between screens; drink water
├─ Energy management: Front-load hardest interviews (9-10 AM); easier ones 3-4 PM
│   (Prevent end-of-day fatigue from affecting quality assessment)

FEB 13 (Day 4 - CRUNCH):
├─ Pressure level: HIGHEST (all offers due, scorecards finalized, decisions immovable)
├─ Mood: Exhausted, potentially overwhelmed
├─ Typical issue: Multiple negotiations happening simultaneously; time pressure
├─ Coping: Delegate to collaborator if possible; take structured breaks
├─ Energy management: Strategic caffeine (coffee 9 AM, 1 PM; avoid 3 PM for sleep)
│   Take 30min walk at 12 PM (clear head before afternoon negotiations)
│   No work after 8 PM (sleep is critical for Day 4 clarity)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRESSURE MANAGEMENT TACTICS:

1. DECISION FATIGUE (you're making 6+ hiring decisions in 4 days)
   └─ Mitigation: Pre-make easy decisions (who gets backup tier contact? when?)
                  Batch similar decisions (all scoring at once, not scattered)
                  Use DECISION TREES to avoid re-deciding same scenario

2. NEGOTIATION FATIGUE (counter-offers deplete mental energy)
   └─ Mitigation: Remember your BATNA (backup candidates exist; you have options)
                  Use talking points script (don't improvise; stick to framework)
                  Take 10min break between negotiation calls (reset mind)

3. REJECTION SENSITIVITY (if early candidates decline, mood dips)
   └─ Mitigation: Reframe rejection as "filtering for quality, not personal"
                  Celebrate early acceptances (if Contractor 3 says YES, note WIN)
                  Remember: 53% expected success rate; 47% rejections are normal

4. TEAM MORALE (after first rejection, team energy dips)
   └─ Mitigation: Daily standup (10 min): "Here's what we won, here's the plan"
                  Don't dwell on rejections (acknowledge, move on)
                  Celebrate signings publicly (morale boost)

5. TIMELINE PRESSURE (Feb 17 kickoff is immovable)
   └─ Mitigation: Remember decision trees have contingencies
                  Backup candidates pre-identified (no starting from scratch)
                  DEFER is acceptable outcome (Jan 24 is okay; Mar 3 is okay)
                  Feb 17 is goal, not fate

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

PRESSURE RELIEF PROTOCOLS:

At 3 PM each day (fatigue peak):
├─ Take 15-min walk (no screens)
├─ Get water/snack
├─ Review that day's wins in DECISION LOG
└─ Reset focus for next task block

At 6 PM each day:
├─ STOP RECRUITING (don't work past 6 PM)
├─ Don't check emails after 6 PM
├─ Go home mentally (psychological shutdown matters)
└─ Return fresh the next morning

If stuck on decision (analysis paralysis at 4 PM):
├─ Use DECISION TREE (don't re-think; execute tree)
├─ If tree doesn't fit: Ask collaborator for 10-min input (get external perspective)
├─ Make decision within 2h of trigger (no overnight delays)
└─ Move on (don't second-guess after decision made)

If multiple rejections (>2 in single day):
├─ Activate BACKUP PLAYBOOK immediately (don't delay)
├─ Call Executive Sponsor by 5 PM same day (inform, don't hide)
├─ Remind team: "Backup plan is solid; we have contingencies"
└─ Emotionally: Rejection is data (market conditions), not personal failure
```

### If a Rejection Happens (Post-Rejection Communication)

```
❌ WRONG WAY (Demoralizes team):
Eric to team: "Bad news, the top Collaborator candidate turned us down. He got
another offer. This is concerning. Let me go to the backup list..."
[Team mood: Anxious, worried project won't happen]

✓ RIGHT WAY (Maintains confidence):
Eric to team: "Quick update: Primary Collaborator candidate declined—he took a
role at a startup with equity incentive. Totally understandable. We have strong
backup candidate; I'm calling them this afternoon. Expected to have signed offer
by EOD tomorrow. This is normal recruiting rhythm; we're still on track."
[Team mood: Confident, informed, no panic]

PSYCHOLOGY:
├─ Normalize rejections ("normal recruiting rhythm")
├─ Explain reason (removes anxiety; makes it about their choice, not our fault)
├─ Reference backup (shows preparedness)
├─ State timeline (gives control back; clear next steps)
└─ Reaffirm plan (no panic, no scope reduction, just adjust)

POST-REJECTION CHECKLIST:
├─ [ ] Understand reason (build competitive intelligence)
├─ [ ] Contact backup candidate within 1 hour
├─ [ ] Update team by EOD same day (don't let rumors form)
├─ [ ] Reaffirm plan: "Still targeting Feb 17 kickoff. Small delay possible but plan is solid."
└─ [ ] Celebrate any acceptances that day (balance narrative)
```

```
ELITE EXECUTION IS NOT:
├─ ❌ Aggressive pressure on candidates ("You must decide today!")
├─ ❌ Cutting corners on quality assessment (rush to fill positions)
├─ ❌ Hiding bad news from team (transparency builds trust)
├─ ❌ Losing composure under pressure (emotional dysregulation creates panic)

ELITE EXECUTION IS:
├─ ✓ Psychological awareness (understand candidate/team decision-making)
├─ ✓ Preparation depth (decision trees, backup playbooks pre-built)
├─ ✓ Timing precision (calling/offering in optimal windows)
├─ ✓ Composed pressure management (stress is energy, not crisis)
├─ ✓ Transparent communication (team knows plan A, B, C)
├─ ✓ Flexible contingency (deferral is acceptable; quality is immovable)
└─ ✓ Self-awareness (know your BATNA, your limits, your decision authority)

THE ELITE PRINCIPLE:
"Speed is secondary to quality. We have backup plans for every failure.
We move fast, but not frantically. We're confident because we're prepared."
```
