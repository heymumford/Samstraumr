## PART 3: COMPETITIVE RESPONSE FRAMEWORK

### If Candidate Says: "I Got a Google Offer"

```
ACKNOWLEDGE & REFRAME:

Acknowledge: "Google is a great company. Congrats on the offer. I assume it's senior/well-compensated?"

Reframe 1 (Publication):
"Here's what's different: At Google, you'll run A/B tests on incrementally better features.
Here, you're designing experiments for a novel research direction. By June, you'll have
3 first-author papers in OOPSLA tier venues. Google researchers write 1 paper every 2 years.
Career-wise, June publication from Samstraumr beats 2 years at Google in terms of resume."

Reframe 2 (Autonomy):
"Google pays more. But you're executing someone else's hypothesis. Here, you design the science.
That autonomy is worth money to most researchers."

Reframe 3 (Exit):
"In 2 years at Google, you'll be further in but not fundamentally different. In 20 weeks here,
you exit with 3 novel publications. That's a different trajectory."

CLOSE:
"I'm not asking you to turn down Google. I'm asking you to consider: Which 6 months would
you rather have on your resume in 5 years? Running other people's tests, or designing novel
research with your name on it?"
```

---

### If Candidate Says: "I Want a Full-Time Role"

```
ACKNOWLEDGE & REFRAME:

Acknowledge: "I get it. Full-time means stability, benefits, no cliff at end."

Reframe 1 (Defined Endpoint is Feature):
"Full-time sounds safe, but it's actually riskier. You're locked in for years. At Samstraumr,
20 weeks means you exit from a position of strength: 3 publications, novel expertise. Then you
choose your next move. Full-time, you're stuck if the company pivots or shrinks."

Reframe 2 (Re-Entry Strength):
"After Samstraumr, you re-enter the market as a published researcher. That's STRONGER than
entering as someone's employee. You can negotiate better next role because you've shipped
research-grade work."

Reframe 3 (Optionality):
"20 weeks gives you options: Return to postdoc, join startup, consult, go for professor role.
Full-time locks you down. Options are worth something."

CLOSE:
"I'm offering you the rare chance to work part-of-the-career differently. Build credentials
that let you choose your next move. That's actually more stable than full-time job security."
```

---

### If Candidate Says: "The Salary is Low"

```
ACKNOWLEDGE & REFRAME:

Acknowledge: "You're right, $60K for 20 weeks is below market. But let me put the value prop
in front of you."

Reframe 1 (Annualized):
"$60K for 20 weeks = $156K annualized (on the table for that period). Google is higher, I know.
But consider total value, not just cash."

Reframe 2 (Publication Value):
"Those 3 papers are worth $X in career capital. If you'd need to spend 2 years at a university
to earn the same publications, what's the economic value of 20 weeks vs. 2 years? Opportunity
cost says Samstraumr is cheaper."

Reframe 3 (Negotiation):
"If publication + autonomy appeal to you, let's talk about bumping the salary. What would make
this compelling? $3.5K/week? $3.2K? Let's find a number that works."

CLOSE:
"I'm not saying Samstraumr is the highest-paid opportunity. I'm saying the total package—
salary + publication + autonomy + novel research—is hard to beat. What number would make it
work for you?"
```

## PART 4: FOMO MANUFACTURING (Ethical Version)

### The Scarcity Frame (True, Not Manipulative)

```
COLLABORATOR RECRUITMENT:
├─ "We're hiring 1 collaborator. We have 6 strong candidates."
├─ "Interviews are Feb 12 afternoon."
├─ "We need a decision by EOD Feb 13."
├─ Implication: "If you don't move fast, we're hiring the other candidate."
│
└─ This is TRUE: We will move fast; one person wins. Scarcity is real.

Pitch: "This is a 20-spot opportunity. We're moving quickly to fill it. If you want it, you
need to commit this week. We can't leave positions open past Feb 13."
```

### The Network Effect (Real & Sustainable)

```
POSITIONING:
├─ "First hire (Collaborator) will likely influence second hire (Contractor 2)"
├─ "If Collaborator recommends a writer, we weight that heavily"
├─ "Top talent attracts top talent"
│
└─ Reality: This is TRUE. A great Collaborator will attract strong contractors + future research partners.

Pitch (to top candidates): "We're building a small, high-caliber team. The Collaborator we hire
will shape the contractor hires. If you're that person, you get to influence who else joins.
That's rare in most roles."
```

### The Novelty Frame (Create FOMO for Future Scarcity)

```
POSITIONING:
├─ "Consciousness-aware systems is emerging field"
├─ "In 2-3 years, every company will explore this"
├─ "Right now, it's rare expertise"
├─ "In 5 years, it's commodity"
│
└─ Reality: This is likely TRUE (or at least defensible).

Pitch: "I'm betting consciousness-aware research becomes mainstream. You could be one of
the first 10 people globally to publish on it. That expertise is valuable while it's rare.
Wait 3 years, everyone will know this domain. Join now, be a pioneer."
```

## PART 7: ANTI-POSITIONING (Avoid These Frames)

```
DON'T SAY:
├─ "This is a startup" (sounds risky)
├─ "We're exploring consciousness, maybe it works" (sounds uncertain)
├─ "It's kind of like a gig" (diminishes credibility)
├─ "We'll figure it out as we go" (sounds unplanned)
└─ "You'll wear many hats" (code for scope creep)

DO SAY:
├─ "This is a defined-scope research project" (sounds professional)
├─ "We're pioneering consciousness-aware architecture" (sounds ambitious)
├─ "It's a 20-week intensive sprint" (sounds focused)
├─ "The full roadmap is locked" (sounds planned)
└─ "You have clear role + decision authority" (sounds structured)
```
